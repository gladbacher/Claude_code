You pay a standing charge every day — even on days you use nothing. Ever asked why?

Wholesale gas prices are real, and global. But between the gas and your direct debit sits a stack of monopolies, levies, and other people's failures — all charged to you. This series takes your bill apart, one line at a time.

One honest concession, up front

Some of your bill is real cost — gas is a global commodity and Britain buys a lot of it. This series never pretends otherwise. The argument is about everything stacked on top of that: regulated returns, flat-rate charges, and failure costs socialised onto the people least able to carry them. Conceding what's true is what makes the rest undeniable.
