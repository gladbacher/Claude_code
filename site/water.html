Your bill went up. The sewage went in the river. The dividends went out.

Privatised water was sold to the public debt-free. It's now tens of billions in debt — and the gap between those two facts is the story of every spill, every hosepipe ban, and every bill rise since.

Spill → company → dividend. Every episode traces one incident all the way to the balance sheet — who owns the company, what they took out, what they didn't put in.

What this series shows you

  • Where your bill actually goesDebt interest and dividends versus pipes and treatment works, with the companies' own filings as the source.
  • The spill data, read properlyWhat the Event Duration Monitoring numbers actually say — including the difference between legal storm overflows and the breaches they hide among.
  • Who owns your waterThe private equity funds, sovereign wealth funds, and overseas pension schemes on the other end of your direct debit.
  • The translation service"Record investment." "Blame the rain." "Blame wet wipes." What the official lines mean once you attach the numbers.
  • Your river, specificallyEvery episode ends with a way to check the waterway nearest you — and what to ask your MP about it.

The rules this series runs on

Companies get named — from Environment Agency enforcement records, Ofwat decisions, and their own accounts, never from hearsay. Legal discharges and illegal ones are never conflated; that distinction is exactly where the industry hopes you'll get sloppy. Health claims are sourced or not made. Anger with receipts, every week.
