How a rumour gets graded

  • Source tierWho originated it — not who repeated it. A "here we go" and a "reports suggest" are not the same species.
  • Agent fingerprintsContract running down? Story drops the week before talks? One outlet, no follow-up? Noted.
  • Financial plausibilityThe fee against the club's actual PSR headroom, the wages against the actual structure, and whether someone needs a sale before June 30.
  • Squad logicDoes the buying club need the position, or does the story just need clicks?
  • The recycler checkSome rumours come back every window like swallows. We keep a list.

The ledger

Every grade is public, dated, and permanent. When a rumour resolves — confirmed, died, or recycled — the ledger gets updated and the hit rate recalculated, on camera. When we're wrong, that goes on camera too. Nobody else in this space keeps score on themselves. That's exactly why you should ask why not.

Plus, every episode: one piece of transfer finance — amortisation, academy "pure profit" sales, sell-on clauses — explained in sixty seconds, so each window makes you permanently harder to fool.
