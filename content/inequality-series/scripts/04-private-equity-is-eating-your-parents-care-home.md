# Script 4 — "Private Equity Is Eating Your Parents' Care Home"

**Series position:** Week 4 — the most visceral, concrete example. Temperature: highest of the series.
**Length:** ~90 seconds. Direct to camera, intercut with care home exteriors (no people, no interiors).
**CTA level:** Ask your MP about private equity in social care.

---

## Script

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:08]**
When your mum goes into a care home, you'll think you're paying for her care. A lot of what you're paying for is somebody's leveraged buyout. Let me walk you through it.

**[THE PLAYBOOK — 0:08–0:40]**
A significant share of Britain's care home sector is owned by private equity. And there's a standard playbook. Step one: buy the operator. Step two: charge it management fees — the company pays its own owner for the privilege of being owned. Step three: sell the buildings and lease them back, so the homes now pay rent on property they used to own. Step four: load the company with debt, because the interest is someone's return. Step five: when the structure can't carry its own weight — administration. Residents and staff get the chaos. The fund has already been paid.

*[Number card: "Fees → out. Property → sold. Debt → loaded. Then: administration."]*

**[WHO PAYS — 0:40–1:05]**
Now connect it to *your* balance sheet. The care settlement in this country is: your parents' assets go first, the state steps in after. Which means the house your parents spent thirty years paying off is sold — and the proceeds flow into that structure. Their home equity becomes fee income, rent, and debt service. The inheritance becomes a line in a fund's quarterly report. This is the machine, end to end: a family's biggest asset, converted into investor returns, at the most vulnerable moment of their lives.

**[CTA — 1:05–1:30]**
Last week I asked for your MP's name. This week, use it. Write to them — one question, this question: *"What proportion of care homes in this constituency are owned by private equity, and what happens to residents when one of them goes into administration?"* They will not have an answer ready. That's the point. Make them go and find out. Comment "sent" when you've done it.

## Production notes

- Cutaways: care home exteriors, an estate agent window, a SOLD sign. Nothing staged, nothing with faces.
- The five-step playbook is the clip-out segment — could run as its own short.
- Deliver "The fund has already been paid" flat. Don't sell it. It sells itself.
- Legal note for production: describe the model and the sector pattern; do not name a specific operator on screen without source material checked.
